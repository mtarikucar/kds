# QR Codes

**Menu path:** Menu & Tables → QR Codes &nbsp;•&nbsp; **URL:** `/admin/qr-codes`
**Access:** ADMIN, MANAGER  •  **Plan:** All plans

## Purpose

The QR Codes screen **generates, downloads, and prints** the QR codes your guests scan with their phones to open your menu. There are two kinds: a single **restaurant-wide QR** for the whole venue, and individual **per-table QR codes**. You also design the look of the QR menu (color, typography, layout, logo) from here.

<Callout type="info">
  This is the admin screen that **generates** the codes. The menu a guest actually
  sees after scanning is a separate screen — see **[QR Menu (customer)](/en/admin-guide/qr-menu)**.
</Callout>

## How QR codes work

The screen is split into two tabs: **QR Codes** and **Design Settings**. The three cards at the top show the current counts: total codes, table QR codes, and the restaurant QR.

<Steps>
### Restaurant QR Code

A single QR for the whole venue. Whoever scans it lands directly on your menu's home page; it isn't tied to a specific table. It's a good fit for the storefront, the door, a business card, or social media.

### Per-Table QR Codes

When **"Enable table QR codes"** is turned on under Design Settings, a separate QR is generated for every table you've defined. A guest who scans one of these goes **straight to that table's menu**, so ordering and self-pay are bound to the correct table. If you haven't defined any tables yet, this section appears empty — add tables first from the **[Tables](/en/admin-guide/tables)** screen.
</Steps>

QR codes are **branch-scoped**: the branch you're viewing in the top branch selector determines which table codes are listed.

## Where each code points

Every QR carries your menu's address: `…/qr-menu/<restaurant-no>` (table codes also include the relevant table). Use the **copy** button under a code to put that address on your clipboard, or **share** to send it straight from your phone.

<Callout type="warning">
  Once you print a code and place it on a table, the restaurant number and the table
  link stay fixed — as long as the address doesn't change, you never need to regenerate
  printed codes. Changing the color or design doesn't affect the address; it only changes
  how the opened menu looks.
</Callout>

## Downloading and printing

You can download each code individually from its own card, or use the bulk actions.

- **Size selection:** every card offers three preset sizes — **Table card** (150×150), **Standard** (300×300), and **Poster** (600×600).
- **Format:** for individual downloads you can choose **PNG**, **SVG** (vector, for lossless scaling), or **PDF**.
- **Download all:** the button on the bulk actions card downloads every code in the list, one after another, as PNG.
- **Print table QR page:** opens all table QRs — each with its table label — on a single print-ready page (grid layout) and launches the print dialog.

<Callout type="info">
  Bulk downloads arrive one at a time, in short intervals, so the files don't get mixed up.
  Because printing opens a new tab, you may need to allow your browser's **pop-up blocker**.
</Callout>

## Design Settings

The **Design Settings** tab edits how the menu looks when a guest scans the QR, with a live preview. Settings are on the left; the QR and menu preview on the right update instantly. There are five sub-tabs:

- **Templates** — apply ready-made look presets with one tap.
- **Colors** — primary, secondary, and background color (preset themes included).
- **Typography** — font and restaurant logo upload (image up to 5 MB).
- **Layout** — list / grid / compact arrangement, products per row, and price-description-image display.
- **QR Style** — toggle table QRs on/off and set the message printed under the table code (e.g. "Scan to see the menu"). The QR pattern uses your chosen primary and background colors.

Changes are written with **"Save all changes"**; **"Reset to default"** returns to factory settings. All prices in the menu are shown in **₺ (TRY)**.

<Callout type="info">
  Plan limits only hide the interface; the real authorization check lives on the server.
  Since QR code generation is enabled on all plans, you won't see an upgrade barrier on
  this screen.
</Callout>
