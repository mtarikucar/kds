# Delivery Platforms

**Menu path:** Settings → Online Ordering Platforms &nbsp;•&nbsp; **URL:** `/admin/settings/online-orders`
**Access:** ADMIN, MANAGER  •  **Plan:** plans that include the `deliveryIntegration` feature

## Purpose

This screen connects your restaurant to the **Getir Yemek, Yemeksepeti, Trendyol Yemek and Migros Yemek** platforms. Once a connection is live, orders from those platforms land straight on your kitchen display (KDS); you can map your products to the platform and push your menu, and you can open or close the store on the platform's side. You manage the whole flow from a single page, and if something goes wrong you can trace it in the activity log on that same page.

<Callout type="info">
  The page always shows all four platform cards. Until you open a card and enter
  its credentials, that platform stays in the **"Not configured"** state and
  receives no orders.
</Callout>

## Connecting a platform

Each platform is listed as an expandable card. Tapping a card's header opens its credentials form.

<Steps>
### Step 1 — Enter the restaurant identifier

In the **Restaurant ID (`remoteRestaurantId`)** field at the top of the form, enter the store/branch number the platform issued to you. Incoming orders and webhook calls are matched to your business through this identifier.

### Step 2 — Enter the credential keys

Each platform expects different fields (`*` is required):

- **Getir Yemek:** App Secret Key\*, Restaurant Secret Key\*
- **Yemeksepeti:** Client ID\*, Client Secret\*, Chain Code, POS Vendor ID
- **Trendyol Yemek:** API Version (`v1` or `v2`), Username, Password, Integrator ID (v2), Integrator Secret (v2)
- **Migros Yemek:** API Key\*

Use the eye icon next to the secret fields to confirm what you typed. The keys are stored on the server and stay hidden when you return to the screen.

### Step 3 — Save and test the connection

The **Save** button appears as soon as you make a change. After saving, use **Test Connection** to verify on the platform's side that the keys are valid. The test performs a real sign-in attempt; if it fails, review the keys or the restaurant identifier.

### Step 4 — Turn the integration on

Flip the toggle in the card header. If any required field is empty, the system warns you and keeps the card open. Once enabled, the status badge changes to **"Connected"** and orders begin to flow.
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
  Creating, updating and deleting a configuration, and pushing the menu, are
  **ADMIN**-only actions. **MANAGER** users can view the cards and the activity
  log, run a connection test, and open or close the store — but they cannot
  change the keys.
</Callout>

## How order sync works

The platforms operate in different ways, and each card's description notes which:

- **Yemeksepeti** — webhook-based (real time). The platform pushes orders to our server instantly.
- **Trendyol Yemek** — a mix of webhook and polling.
- **Getir Yemek** — polling-based (roughly every 15 sec).
- **Migros Yemek** — polling-based (roughly every 20 sec).

When **Auto Accept** is on, an incoming order is accepted without any manual step and sent to the kitchen; when it's off, you have to confirm each order by hand. The **Last poll** and **Last menu sync** timestamps at the bottom of the card let you confirm the flow is live.

<Callout type="info">
  **Open / Close store:** this button, which appears on the card while the
  integration is on, marks your restaurant as open or closed only on that
  platform's side. It's handy for pausing new orders during a rush — you can close
  the store temporarily without turning the integration off.
</Callout>

## Menu mapping

To push your menu to a platform, you must link each of your products to its counterpart on that platform. A mapping is established between a **product** in the system and the platform's **external item ID** (`externalItemId`). The menu sync pushes only **mapped** products and writes each product's name, price (₺) and stock status to the platform. If you mark a product as "out of stock" in HummyTummy, that status can be reflected on the platform too.

<Callout type="warning">
  On a given platform, an external item ID can be mapped only once; the system
  rejects duplicates. Create your products first on the
  **[Menu](https://help.hummytummy.com/en/admin-guide/menu)** screen, then map them here.
</Callout>

## Activity log and error handling

The **Activity Log** at the bottom of the page shows recent order and sync operations. You can filter by all / successful / failed, and expand any row to see its details. Personal data in the raw payload of incoming orders is masked before it is written to the log.

As errors accumulate on a platform, the card badge switches to a **"Error"** warning. If the error count reaches **10**, the circuit breaker trips, the badge becomes **"Circuit Open"**, and the integration is turned off automatically. When this happens, open the card and read the **Last error** message, fix the credentials, verify with **Test Connection**, and then turn the integration back on.

<Callout type="info">
  The plan restriction is enforced not only in the UI but on the server too: if
  your plan doesn't include the `deliveryIntegration` feature, this screen's
  actions are rejected. Since incoming orders enter the kitchen flow, we recommend
  completing your **[KDS (Kitchen Display)](https://help.hummytummy.com/en/admin-guide/kitchen)** and
  **[Menu](https://help.hummytummy.com/en/admin-guide/menu)** setup before you start using this screen.
</Callout>
